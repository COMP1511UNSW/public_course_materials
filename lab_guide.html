Lab Locations

{{course_code}} labs are held in Brass or Strings labs on level 3 of the J17 building

CSE labs are spread over several buildings. Find your way to the labs with the Map of the CSE labs

Logging in

You are allocated a CSE account. Your CSE username is an alphanumeric name which is your zID for students starting uni in 2014 or a combination of your firstname lastname and random three digits at the end e.g. abcd123

You log into your CSE account with your zID and zPass.

The default desktop environment is Fluxbox. You may choose a different one by selecting a session on the Login Window. Fluxbox is a lightweight desktop environment. You may choose Gnome for a more user-friendly environment with point and click or drag,

Lab Environment

The CSE labs use the Linux operating system rather than Windows or Macintosh.

There are two Terminal windows when you log in with Fluxbox. If there are none on your desktop, find the Terminal application from the menu (right-click for Fluxbox). You will be using the Terminal window to run Linux commands.

To find out your CSE username type whoami or acc on a Terminal window.

You will have a small amount of storage in your CSE home directory. You may bring a USB drive to uni to transfer your files to and from home. If you choose Gnome as your desktop environment, the file manager automatically detects the USB device. If you use Fluxbox, the following commands will mount, list the files stored and unmount or eject the USB device. 

priv usb mount 1
ls /mnt/usb/1
priv usb umount 1
More information on using USB devices in the labs on the CSE Taggi

Submitting your lab work

The exact command to submit your lab work will be given to you each week.

In general, to submit work named assignment,
  • log into your CSE account
  • cd to the directory containing the files for assignment
  • type on a Terminal window the command
    give {{course_account}} assignment files...

It is good practice to check that your submission is accepted with this command: ~{{course_account}}/bin/classrun -check assignment

And finally to collect your marked submission, ~{{course_account}}/bin/classrun -collect assignment

At the end of your class

  • Save your work. You should save your work at regular intervals anyway.
  • Take out your USB device from the computer, especially if it contains private information or assignment work.
  • Log off.
